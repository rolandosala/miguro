Understanding the Japanese Particle は (wa)

What is は (wa)?

In Japanese, は is one of the most common particles you'll come across. Although it's written as は (ha) in hiragana, it's pronounced "wa" when used as a particle. Its main job? To mark the topic of a sentence.

Think of it like this: は tells us what the sentence is about.

Basic Sentence Structure

[Topic] + は + [Information about the topic]

Example:

わたし は がくせい です。
Watashi wa gakusei desu.
➡ I am a student.

  • わたし (watashi) = I
  • は (wa) = topic marker
  • がくせい (gakusei) = student
  • です (desu) = polite ending meaning "is/am/are"

Explanation: "Watashi wa" tells us the sentence is about *me*. What about me? I'm a student.

More Examples

Example:

にほんご は むずかしい です。
Nihongo wa muzukashii desu.
➡ Japanese is difficult.

  • にほんご (Nihongo) = Japanese (language)
  • むずかしい (muzukashii) = difficult

Explanation: The topic is Japanese, and we’re saying it’s difficult.

は vs. が? (Quick Tip)

Beginners often confuse は and が, since both can seem similar. A simple way to remember the difference at first:

  • は (wa) = Topic
  • が (ga) = Subject (often introduces new or important information)

Example 1:

わたし は たなか です。
Watashi wa Tanaka desu.
➡ As for me, I’m Tanaka. (Topic = "me")

Example 2:

だれ が たなか です か？
Dare ga Tanaka desu ka?
➡ Who is Tanaka? (Subject = "Who")

When to Use は (wa)

Use は when:

  • You're introducing a new topic
  • You want to talk about something or someone
  • You’re contrasting two things

Example of contrast:

さかな は すき です。でも にく は すきじゃない です。
Sakana wa suki desu. Demo niku wa suki janai desu.
➡ I like fish. But I don’t like meat.

Final Tip: は ≠ "is"

While in English we say “A is B”, in Japanese は doesn’t mean “is”. It just shows what we’re talking about. The です (desu) part usually does the job of “is” or “am”.

Practice Time! Try Making Your Own:

How would you say:

  • "As for Tokyo, it’s big." → Tokyo wa _____ desu.
  • "As for me, I like sushi." → Watashi wa sushi ga _____ desu.

Answers: ōkii for "big", and suki for "like"
